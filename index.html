Perm-36: political prison

Perm-36 was a Soviet correction labor camp in the large Soviet Union prison camp system (Gulag). International Memorial estimates that more than 11 million people passed through the Gulag camps (about 1 million were shot); other sources suggest that the numbers may be even higher.

It is located near the village of Kuchino, 100 km northeast of the city of Perm in Russia. In Soviet times it was surrounded by the similar camps Perm-35 and Perm-37.

Perm-36 stands out among other camps:

  • In the 1970s, it became the large camp for political prisoners designated for "especially dangerous state criminals", mostly Soviet dissidents (including members of the Ukrainian, Latvian, Lithuanian, and Estonian nationalist movements, Jews forbidden to leave the Soviet Union (refuseniks), etc.).
  • It is the only well-preserved Gulag-era camp complex in the world. In the 1990s, a museum was opened on the camp's territory.
